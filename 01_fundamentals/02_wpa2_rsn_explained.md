# WPA2 and RSN Explained

This section explains what **RSN** and **WPA2** actually mean when you see them
during a Wi-Fi scan.

---

## What is WPA2?

**WPA2 (Wi-Fi Protected Access 2)** is a security standard that provides:
- Authentication (prove you know the key)
- Encryption (protect traffic confidentiality)
- Integrity (prevent tampering)

In many labs, WPA2 is used with a **pre-shared key (PSK)**.

---
